My Little Miracles

I always knew that I wanted to have children and felt that my main purpose on this earth was to be a mother. Being in a same sex relationship, we did not have the luxury of having children the “old fashioned” way. We looked into different avenues, but most were so expensive and uncertain that we weren’t sure if it was ever going to happen. One day in April of 2010, I received a call from my mother saying that one of my cousins had just had a baby girl and the baby was being taken by Child Protective Services. My cousin was not exactly stable. She was homeless and tested positive for drugs at the time of birth. She called my mom hoping that I would take the baby and raise her myself. We didn’t even have to think about it, she was already ours. Just a year and a half later, in October of 2011, we received another call that my cousin had just given birth to another baby girl that was being removed from her custody. Long story short, less than eight hours after that phone call, we were parents to two girls. The adoption processes were long and extremely stressful, but I would not change a single thing. It was worth every fear and every tear because I get to wake up every day and have these two beautiful, funny, smart creatures call me mom mom.
